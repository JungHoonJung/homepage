About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate/researcher in Physics at the University of Seoul. My research focuses on understanding the structure and dynamics of complex systems, utilizing tools from network science, nonlinear dynamics, and statistical physics.

I am particularly interested in real-world, spatially embedded networks and higher-order network structures (e.g., simplicial complexes, hypergraphs). My recent doctoral work established novel frameworks for identifying local congestion events and analyzing urban traffic congestion as a complex contagion process, seeking to capture topological loop structures and hysteresis in urban transportation networks.

Research Interests

  • Collective phenomena
  • Cascading failure
  • Network structure
  • Spreading phenomena
  • Topological data analysis
  • Human mobility networks
  • Urban physics
